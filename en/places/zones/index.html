    Leça da Palmeira Lighthouse

    2 Leça da Palmeira Lighthouse Matosinhos Infraestruturas 1900 – 1950 Eng. José Joaquim Peres 1926 Belonging to the sea, a lighthouse is a construction made to be detected at a distance and to see further away. It also serves to warn the seamen in order for them to arrive to shore safely. In a time of blind confidence in GPS and the sophisticated telecommunication systems, it is the last resort to guarantee a safe navigation, in the eventuality of all else falling. To visit the Leça Lighthouse and go up its almost 50 meters of height is like stepping into another time, in a watchmaker’s piece, where old mechanisms are immaculately maintained by the continuous zeal of lighthouse keepers.

    Casa da Arquitectura - Real Vinícola

    10 Casa da Arquitectura - Real Vinícola Matosinhos Equipamentos 1900 – 1950 2000 – 2017 Eng. António da Silva / Guilherme Vaz 1901 / 2017 Sat 21h, Arq. Nuno Sampaio / Sun 17h, V. Pres. Fernando Rocha e Músico Pedro Guedes The Real Companhia Vinícola building dates from the end of the 19th Century. It was the first construction to house, almost in its entirety, the rectangle drawn in the Matosinhos Plan by Licínio Guimarães, that defined a New York grid to implement the canning factories and other industries. It was also the last building to resist the recent unbridled occupation of the interior of the blocks. A classified building, recovered just in time by Guilherme Machado Vaz from the Matosinhos Municipality, it will be the new home of the Casa da Arquitectura. A house to better preserve, study and promote the architectural archives entrusted to it. It will also be the house of other songs, other arts and a cosmopolitan place, that the city will appreciate.

    Serra do Pilar Barrack

    52 Serra do Pilar Barrack Vila Nova de Gaia Equipamentos Até Séc. XVI Frei Brás de Barros 1538 The installation of this barrack dates back to the beginning of the 16th Century, almost simultaneously with the construction of the Monastery. During these almost 500 years, it was of course involved in the military conflicts that most threatened the “Cidade Invicta” [never-beaten city] character that Porto is precisely most proud of. For its strategic location, it was decisive in the expulsion of Napoleon’s invading troops and in the fight that opposed liberals to absolutists. The origin of this barrack merges with the one of the monastery that, before having the name it is know for today, had also the name of São Salvador do Mundo and Salvador do Porto [Saint Saviour of the world and Porto Saviour]. The strategic position it occupies, so effective in times of war, offers a beautiful view over the city that in these more quiet times that we live in, is good to savour.
